## The scenario: solar-conjunction optical blackout

During Earth–Sun–Mars conjunction the direct 1550 nm line-of-sight passes
through the Sun's corona. Solar-plasma scintillation collapses the optical
link quality below the agent's **0.3 forward threshold** — the link is
effectively *closed*.

| Path | Band | Conjunction quality | Status | Reward (conjunction) |
|------|------|--------------------:|:------:|--------------------:|
| Direct Mars → Earth | 1550 nm optical | 0.05 | **CLOSED** | −1.44 |
| Mars → **ES-L4** → Earth | Ka-band RF | 0.65 | **OPEN** | −0.20 |
| Mars → **ES-L5** → Earth | Ka-band RF | 0.60 | **OPEN** | −0.20 |

*Rewards computed with the real weights: R = +1.0·delivery − 0.001·delay −
0.1·hops. Source: `run_simulation.py --module 4`.*

## How AETHERIX recovers — automatically

1. **Detect** — the RL agent's Q-value for the optical path collapses (the
   link quality 0.05 < 0.3 threshold → no delivery reward → negative return).
2. **Re-route** — the agent, in exploit mode, selects the highest-Q neighbour:
   **ES-L4 (Ka-band RF)**, which sits at 60° solar elongation and avoids the
   corona.
3. **Prioritise** — the policy engine fires two rules simultaneously:
   - **P0 EMERGENCY** → `emergency_fast_path` → *forward* on the best
     available (Ka-band) link. Mission-critical bundles reach Earth.
   - **P4 BULK** → `deep_space_store` → *store* locally and defer until the
     conjunction window passes. No bandwidth wasted on non-urgent data.

```
    Mars areo-relay ──optical CLOSED──✗──► Earth (direct)
         │
         └──Ka-band OPEN──► ES-L4 relay ──Ka-band──► Earth  ✓ P0 delivered
```

## Why the Lagrange relays make this possible

ES-L4 and ES-L5 sit 60° ahead/behind Earth in its orbit. At that angular
separation they maintain **line-of-sight to Mars around the solar limb** even
at true conjunction — providing 50–70% availability versus **0%** for the
direct Earth–Mars link. No Mars-orbit relay can solve this; the geometry is on
the Earth side.

## Engineering takeaways

- **Defence-in-depth**: the RL agent adapts in real time; the policy engine
  guarantees deterministic P0 pre-emption regardless of agent confidence.
- **Graceful degradation**: throughput drops (optical → RF) but **no
  mission-critical data is lost** — exactly the SLA a Mars mission demands.
